About Andrew

Raised on a Cattle Ranch.
Built in the Courtroom.

Andrew R. Farwell was born and raised near Kirksville, Missouri, on his parents' cattle ranch. He graduated from Kirksville High School and went on to earn his bachelor's degree with honors from the University of Arkansas–Fayetteville in 1999, followed by his law degree from the University of Missouri–Columbia School of Law in 2002.

In December 2003 — just one year out of law school — he opened his own practice in Kirksville. He has been there ever since.

In the 22 years since, Andrew has represented clients in civil and criminal matters in more than 35 county courts across northern and central Missouri. He has argued before the Eastern and Western District Missouri Courts of Appeal, the U.S. Federal District Courts in Missouri, and even litigated a six-week jury trial in Nevada County, California.

He's a past President of the Adair County Bar Association. The Firm maintains an A+ rating with the Better Business Bureau and a 5.0-star client rating across review platforms.

But the credential that matters most isn't on a diploma. It's this: Andrew tries cases. While most of his peers settle every file that crosses their desk, Andrew prepares every case as if a jury will decide it — because sometimes, that's exactly what happens.

Andrew and his wife Allison returned to Kirksville to raise their children in the same community where Andrew grew up. When he represents a client, he's representing a neighbor.
